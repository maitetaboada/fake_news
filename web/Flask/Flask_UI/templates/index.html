Discourse Processing Lab

Comment Moderation and Fake News

What if technology could help with moderating online content?

At the Discourse Processing Lab, we have two focused projects on organizing and moderating online content: the fake news project and the news comment moderation project. In these projects, we apply general methods of text classification ranging from classic machine learning algorithms (e.g., Naive Bayes, Decision Trees, SVMs) to deep learning architectures (e.g., feed-forward, recurrent and convolutional neural networks). The aim of the fake news project is to help news readers to identify bias and misinformation in news articles in a quick and reliable fashion. We have collected a large corpus of news articles from online publishers with different levels of veracity and built classification models based on linguistic cues of deception. Through systematic generalizations across genre and publishers that happen during training of the model, the system can be used to score the likelihood of an unseen article to be deceptive. The news comment moderation project aims to encourage constructive discussion online. This can be done, for example, by promoting the most informative reader comments. As part of this project, we have collected and curated a corpus of about 10,000 opinion articles, together with their more than one million reader comments, from the national newspaper in Canada, The Globe and Mail. We have annotated subsets of this corpus for constructiveness and toxicity. We have also developed computational methods to identify constructive comments, which exploit linguistic features for constructiveness. Our methods can assist in moderation tasks, typically performed by humans, such as organizing and summarizing reader comments.
